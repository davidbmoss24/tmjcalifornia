# Strategy Memo — TMJ California / Dr. Dwight Jennings

_A working brief for the new website, drawn from a full review of the archived 2024 site._

## The opportunity

Dr. Jennings runs an unusual practice that the old website doesn't sell. He is:

- 40 years specialized in dental orthopedics — almost no one in the U.S. has this depth of specialization
- Running a **tertiary referral practice** — patients fly in to Oakland to see him, many travel 50+ miles
- A **non-surgical alternative** for patients who've been told they need orthognathic or TMJ surgery
- A treating clinician for a long list of conditions other dentists don't touch — sleep apnea, movement disorders (Tourette's, Parkinson's, dystonia), chronic headaches, fibromyalgia, autism, and a wide range of "medical mystery" cases
- An active researcher with original peer-reviewed work on the trigeminal-substance P axis

The old site reads like an academic paper. It buries the doctor, hides the patient story, and gives a suffering visitor no clear path to act. That's the opening.

## Positioning

**For patients who have run out of answers**, TMJ California offers a non-surgical, jaw-orthopedic explanation — and a practical treatment — for the chronic, complex, often unexplained symptoms that mainstream medical workups keep missing.

**For clinicians**, Dr. Jennings is the specialist they refer the case they can't crack — backed by 40 years of practice and a published body of research.

The contrarian thesis (jaw misalignment as a hidden driver of systemic illness) is the unique selling proposition. We lead with it confidently, with the science to back it up.

## Audience hierarchy

The site speaks to two audiences in order of priority:

1. **Patients** — primary navigation, hero, every condition page is patient-first. Tone: empathetic, direct, free of dental jargon at the top, deeper as you scroll.
2. **Referring clinicians** — a clearly-labeled "Research & Referrals" section with mechanism papers, published research, the "New Model of Occlusion" PDF, and a referral pathway.

## Message hierarchy (top to bottom)

1. _Headline:_ "When the medical system can't explain your symptoms, the answer may be in your jaw."
2. _The thesis in one paragraph:_ jaw misalignment → trigeminal hypertonia → elevated substance P → systemic dysfunction. Forty years of clinical evidence, published research, and patient outcomes.
3. _Conditions we treat:_ TMJ pain · Chronic headaches & migraines · Sleep apnea · Movement disorders · Facing jaw surgery · Complex/undiagnosed chronic illness.
4. _Why patients travel here:_ 40+ years specialized · tertiary referral practice · non-surgical · published researcher · innovative protocols.
5. _Meet Dr. Jennings:_ humanizing intro with photo, training, and philosophy.
6. _Evidence:_ headline papers, "New Model of Occlusion" download, published research.
7. _Take the first step:_ clear CTA, what to expect, contact options, travel info.

## What's missing that we'll need to commission

For the live site (not blocking the mockup):

- Professional photography — Dr. Jennings, the office, the appliances, treatment in action
- Patient testimonials and full case histories (the old site has just one terse case)
- Practical info — typical treatment length, fees, insurance handling, travel logistics for out-of-state patients
- A "What to expect at your first visit" walkthrough
- The missing PDF papers that didn't survive in the Wayback snapshot — Dr. Jennings should send fresh copies

## Voice

- Confident, direct, evidence-led
- Empathetic at the top of pages, technical at the bottom
- Specific over generic — "40 years," "tertiary referral," "fly in to Oakland" — these details carry the message
- Never apologetic about the contrarian thesis — but always sourced

## What we're avoiding

- Stock dental-clinic photography of smiling families (this isn't that kind of practice)
- Sales-y "schedule your free consultation today!" energy
- Any visual that signals "general dentistry" — Dr. Jennings is a specialist, the design must feel like one
- Watered-down medical disclaimer language that hides what makes him different
