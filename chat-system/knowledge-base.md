## THE MECHANISM (in plain English)

Most modern humans have what Dr. Jennings calls a "retruded" jaw — the lower jaw sits too far back into the skull, compressing the temporomandibular joint (TMJ). This is largely a consequence of soft modern diets reducing chewing forces over generations.

When the TMJ is chronically compressed:
1. The **trigeminal nerve** (the densest nerve for facial sensation and chewing-muscle control) becomes hyperactive.
2. Trigeminal C-fibers release **substance P** — a neuropeptide that, unlike most neurotransmitters, has no reuptake mechanism. It accumulates.
3. Elevated substance P binds **NK-1 receptors** throughout the body, opening cell membranes, letting calcium flood in, and triggering neurogenic inflammation. This is well-documented in peer-reviewed literature for asthma, IBD, rheumatoid arthritis, fibromyalgia, and migraine.
4. Sustained substance P elevation correlates clinically with a long list of conditions — chronic headaches, sleep apnea, fibromyalgia, IBS, autoimmune disorders, movement disorders (Tourette's, Parkinson's), mood and addiction syndromes, even some forms of cancer per Dr. Jennings' clinical observation.

Steps 1–3 are textbook neuroscience. Step 4 is **Dr. Jennings' clinical hypothesis**, supported by 40 years of patient observation and a growing peer-reviewed evidence base, but not yet established by controlled trials in every patient population.

**Key research finding:** A 2019 *PLoS One* population study of nearly 25,000 Taiwanese patients found **2.11× elevated TMD prevalence in Parkinson's patients** vs. matched controls — and 4.25× in the first year after Parkinson's diagnosis. The mainstream interpretation is that Parkinson's pathology damages the jaw; Dr. Jennings argues the reverse, that for many patients the jaw came first.

## CONDITIONS DR. JENNINGS TREATS

### TMJ pain and dysfunction
The foundational condition. Jaw pain, clicking, locking, ear pressure, facial pain. Treatment with a precisely fitted removable appliance to realign the jaw resolves most cases without surgery.

### Chronic headaches and migraines
Bite alignment therapy is shown in multiple studies to be approximately **85% effective at reducing chronic headaches** — regardless of headache type. The mechanism: the trigeminal nerve is the source of pain signaling for the face and head; chronic activation drives the release of substance P and CGRP, which are the established mediators of migraine.

### Sleep apnea
Advanced obstructive sleep apnea treatment via jaw orthopedics — for patients who can't tolerate or aren't well-served by CPAP. The mechanism: when the jaw is retruded, the airway behind the tongue narrows; bringing the jaw forward opens it. ~85% of sleep apnea is obstructive in origin.

### Movement disorders
Tourette's, Parkinson's, dystonia, torticollis, scoliosis, gait disorders. The clinical case is built on the dense innervation of the reticular formation (the brain's movement-generation center) by trigeminal proprioceptors, plus the substance-P modulation of movement. Sims & Stack 2009 (Tourette's pilot study, PMID 19241794) and Sims/Stack/Demerjian 2012 (torticollis, PMID 22916671) are the published case-series papers.

### Surgical alternative
Many cases that have been recommended for orthognathic or TMJ surgery can be treated nonsurgically with precision jaw orthopedics. Dr. Jennings' practice uses removable appliances almost exclusively. Surgery rate in his treatment plans: roughly 0%.

### Complex and undiagnosed chronic illness
Fibromyalgia, IBS, autoimmune disorders, mood disorders, neurodegeneration, multiple chemical sensitivity, asthma, allergies. Not every case is jaw-driven, but for patients who've cycled through specialists without answers, the bite is worth a careful look.

## TREATMENT APPROACH (what to expect)

**Initial review:** Phone call or written intake. Dr. Jennings personally reviews each inquiry and tells you honestly whether your case is a fit. No charge.

**Comprehensive evaluation:** In-person, includes detailed history, functional jaw-motion testing, and CBCT imaging where appropriate.

**Treatment, if appropriate:**
- Phase 1 — Diagnostic. A removable appliance (often a "twin block" with ramp guides) is fitted. The jaw is gradually repositioned. Adjustments every 4–6 weeks.
- Phase 2 — Orthodontic finishing. Once the right jaw position is established, the back teeth are grown taller via segmental orthodontics so the new position is self-supporting.
- Phase 3 — Nighttime retention. A neutral bionator-style appliance maintains the position during sleep.

**Typical timeline:** 18–24 months for moderate cases. 2–5 years for severe cases or cases reversing prior bad orthodontics.

**Pace of symptom relief:** Over 50% of pain patients report substantial relief within 48 hours of the first appliance fitting. Full healing of the broader systemic patterns takes the full treatment course.

**Why most TMJ treatment fails elsewhere:** Wrong understanding (treating it as psychosomatic), wrong appliances (flat splints don't work), wrong jaw position chosen, no nighttime appliance to prevent relapse, no long-term retention. Dr. Jennings' 5-minute YouTube video "Why TMJ Treatment Often Fails" lays this out.

**Cost framing:**
- Moderate cases: roughly $7,000–9,000 over the course of treatment.
- Severe cases (e.g. reversing extraction-based orthodontics): up to $15,000–20,000.
- Cost is roughly comparable to standard orthodontics, sometimes slightly more.

**Practical advice for new patients:**
- The first step is a phone call or written intake. Dr. Jennings reviews each one personally.
- For out-of-state patients, the practice can help coordinate diagnostic work into multi-day visits and accepts virtual follow-ups via Zoom for many adjustments after the first phase.

## RESEARCH HIGHLIGHTS BY CONDITION

When someone asks "what does the science say about X" — these are the strongest pointers:

**Migraines / chronic headaches**: trigemino-vascular system (Buzzi & Moskowitz 1992, PMID 1379707); ~85% reduction with bite therapy.

**Parkinson's**: TMD prevalence 2.11× elevated (Chen 2019, PMID 31199837); systematic review confirms (Minervini 2023, PMID 37147766); Thornton 2008 substance-P antagonist reverses experimental Parkinsonism.

**Fibromyalgia**: substance P 3-5× normal in spinal fluid (well-replicated); inflammatory mechanism (O'Connor 2004, PMID 15334652).

**Asthma**: tachykinin / NK-1 receptor antagonism in asthma (Ramalho 2011, PMID 21810226).

**IBS / gut motility**: tachykinins in gastric motility (Schmidt & Holst 2000, PMID 11576394).

**Movement disorders**: Sims & Stack Tourette's pilot study (PMID 19241794); Sims, Stack & Demerjian torticollis paper (PMID 22916671).

**Sleep apnea**: 85% obstructive (Mayo Clinic and standard sleep medicine literature).

**Cognitive impairment / dementia**: mastication and brain function (Ono 2010, J Oral Rehabil); occlusal support and brain function (Okamoto 2011, PMID 21333621).

**Brain blood flow**: shortened dental arch and cerebral regional blood volume (Miyamoto 2009, PMID 19455920).

**Bone metabolism (osteoporosis, healing)**: substance P osteotropic effects (Lerner & Persson 2008, PMID 18622084).

**Glia, neurodegeneration**: Barres 2008 (PMID 18995817) — foundational glial cell paper.
