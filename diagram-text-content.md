## STAGE 01 — STRUCTURAL

### Header block
- **Eyebrow:** STAGE 01 · STRUCTURAL
- **Title (large serif):** Misalignment
- **Subtitle (serif italic):** The jaw retrudes. The TMJ is compressed.

### Annotations on the anatomy
- **Hot-spot label (red):** TMJ COMPRESSION
  - Sub-line: joint loaded above resting
- **Arrow label (red):** mandible retrudes
  - Sub-line: to align teeth

### Data callout
- **Big number (red serif):** 3 mm
- **Label below number:** typical retrusion at presentation

### Body description (3 lines)
A vertical-height deficiency — often inherited, often worsened by extraction-based orthodontics — forces the lower jaw to retrude. The TMJ is compressed. The temporalis muscle works continuously to hold the jaw in a non-resting state.

### Source line (italic, gray, small)
Anatomy: Gray's Anatomy, plate 188 (1918, public domain)

## STAGE 02 — NEURAL

### Header block
- **Eyebrow:** STAGE 02 · NEURAL
- **Title (large serif):** Trigeminal firing
- **Subtitle (serif italic):** Chronic irritation hyperactivates the trigeminal nerve.

### Annotations on the anatomy
- **Hot-spot label (teal):** SEMILUNAR GANGLION
  - Sub-line 1: trigeminal cell bodies — only
  - Sub-line 2: primary sensory bodies in CNS
- **Hot-spot label (teal):** V3 MANDIBULAR BRANCH
  - Sub-line 1: where compression-driven
  - Sub-line 2: C-fibers release substance P

### Data callout
- **Big number (teal serif):** ~100×
- **Label below number:** C-fiber density vs. other nerves

### Body description (3 lines)
The trigeminal carries the densest concentration of unmyelinated C-fibers in the body — the pain afferents that release substance P, a neuropeptide with no reuptake mechanism. It accumulates and lingers.

### Source line (italic, gray, small)
Anatomy: Gray's Anatomy, plate 778 (1918, public domain)

## STAGE 03 — BIOCHEMICAL

### Header block
- **Eyebrow:** STAGE 03 · BIOCHEMICAL
- **Title (large serif):** At the cell membrane
- **Subtitle (serif italic):** Substance P binds NK-1. Calcium floods in. Inflammation follows.

### Labels on the cell illustration
- **Top corner label:** EXTRACELLULAR
- **Bottom corner label:** INTRACELLULAR
- **SP molecule label:** SP
- **SP caption (below molecule):** substance P
- **Receptor label:** NK-1
- **Receptor caption:** receptor
- **Ca²⁺ ion label (on each):** Ca²⁺
- **Right-side annotation:** inflammation

### Data callout
- **Big number (teal serif):** 3–5×
- **Label below number, line 1:** elevated in fibromyalgia
- **Label below number, line 2:** spinal fluid

### Side panel — KEY FACTS (each is a bullet with a sub-line)
- **KEY FACTS** _(header)_
- **• No reuptake** — SP accumulates
- **• NK-1 receptors** — throughout body
- **• Ca²⁺ influx** — cell membrane porosity
- **• Neurogenic** — inflammation
- **• Established** — in IBD, RA, asthma

### Body description (3 lines)
Substance P has no reuptake mechanism — it accumulates in tissue. Binding NK-1 receptors opens cell membranes to calcium influx and triggers neurogenic inflammation. Established in IBD, RA, asthma, fibromyalgia.

### Source line (italic, gray, small)
Mechanism: O'Connor et al., Journal of Cellular Physiology, 2004 (PMID 15334652)

## STAGE 04 — SYSTEMIC

### Header block
- **Eyebrow:** STAGE 04 · SYSTEMIC
- **Title (large serif):** The downstream
- **Subtitle (serif italic):** Inflammation spreads. The body absorbs the cost.

### Hot-spot labels on the body figure (in roughly top-to-bottom order)
1. Migraines / chronic headaches
2. TMJ pain · facial pain
3. Tinnitus · ear pressure
4. Neck pain · forward head posture
5. Sleep apnea (airway)
6. Fibromyalgia · chronic pain
7. IBS · gut motility
8. Autoimmune disorders
9. Movement disorders
10. Mood disorders · addiction

### Data callout
- **Big number (red serif):** 2.11×
- **Label below number:** TMD risk in Parkinson's (PLoS One 2019)

### Body description (3 lines)
Substance P-driven inflammation correlates with the conditions Dr. Jennings sees most often. Whether substance P is causal or correlative in each is an active research question. Bite alignment therapy reduces this constellation in patients who haven't responded to mainstream care.

### Source line (italic, gray, small)
Illustration: original. Conditions list drawn from clinical literature and Dr. Jennings' published work.

## Typography (for matching)

- **Headlines / titles / data numbers:** A transitional serif — the diagrams use DejaVu Serif Bold. Equivalent in design tools: **Cormorant Garamond Bold**, **Crimson Pro Bold**, **EB Garamond Bold**, or **PT Serif Bold**. Choose whichever fits your design system.
- **Eyebrows / labels / data labels:** A clean sans-serif — the diagrams use Lato Bold. Equivalent: **Inter Bold**, **Helvetica Neue Bold**, **Source Sans Pro Bold**.
- **Body descriptions:** Lato Regular at ~16–18px. Equivalent: **Inter Regular**, **Helvetica Neue Regular**, **Source Sans Pro Regular**.
- **Source / footer credits:** Lato Italic at ~13px. Equivalent: any italic of the body sans.

Letter-spacing on the eyebrow labels: about 0.10–0.16em (uppercase, slightly tracked out).
