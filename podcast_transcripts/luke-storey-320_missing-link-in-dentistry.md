**Defining TMJ properly:**

"TMJ stands for temporomandibular joint dysfunction. Everybody's got two TMJs. But the term is not a good description of what we deal with in most cases. A lot of people can have pretty severe craniomandibular dysfunction without any TMJ manifestations — without any clicking, popping, joint pain, or facial pain — but still have substantial craniomandibular dysfunction. For most people, this dysfunction is *covert*, not overt. It's not causing any obvious symptoms, but it leads to a large number of dental, medical, and psychiatric conditions."

"I came across substance P about 25 years ago. I was working with physicians on chronic fatigue and fibromyalgia. I had a case come in for migraines post-whiplash injury. He says, 'Ah, that's substance P.'"

**On the trigeminal pain fiber density:**

"Substance P primarily comes from C fibers — pain fibers in your body. Pain fibers are everywhere in your body except inside your brain. When you push on the skin, pressure is transmitted to the brain via C fibers, which secrete substance P. The C fibers on your eyeball are 400 times as dense as on your skin. The trigeminal nerve has 100 times more dense C fibers than any other nerve in your body. When this system gets overworked and overstimulated, it has a dramatic impact on substance P levels throughout your whole body."

**On the trigeminal mesencephalic nucleus — the punchline:**

"Sensors in your teeth, skin, lips — everywhere — tell the brain where your jaw is in space. Those are called proprioceptors. All the proprioceptors from your trigeminal nerve go to a particular nucleus called the **trigeminal mesencephalic nucleus**. It is composed of the *only* sensory cell bodies in the brain. All the sensory cell bodies for your eyes, ears, and skin are out in the ganglia. But your jaw proprioceptors are *in the brain*. For some reason, developmentally, jaw alignment has very high importance in brain function. That nucleus is paired side-by-side with your locus coeruleus — your sympathetic ganglion. They share a lot of information. So anytime there's jaw misalignment, it puts you into sympathetic overdrive."

**On why jaws have degenerated:**

"In the last 250 years there have been very dramatic shifts in craniofacial structure. They think this is primarily due to industrialized diet — the food we're eating is not hard or tough enough to make us work hard enough chewing. Epigenetic shifts are changing us every generation. 60% of the population now has snoring or sleep apnea by age 60. We're getting very compromised. As that impacts substance P, it has a massive influence on health. A Kaiser study showed people with jaw dysfunction have very high medical utilization rates — for ENT complaints, four times as frequent as non-TMJ patients. It runs the possibility of bankrupting this country in healthcare costs."

**On allergies / Pottenger cat studies:**

**On the trigeminal nerve and cognition / brain blood flow (Luke version):**

"One of the Japanese studies showed that people missing their back molars, when they clench down, on average had **a 40% decrease in brain blood flow**. 40%.

**On sleep apnea:**

**Treatment overview:**

- Examine, integrate medical history
- Refer for CBCT (3D x-ray) of the jaw joint
- Bring the jaw forward, get tip-to-tip in most cases
- Use orthopedic appliance — twin block crozat with little ramp guides
- Patented in 1905. Acrylic pads invented by Irish dentist ~30 years ago
- Asymmetric movement: don't use expansion screws (symmetric only) — use a wire on one tooth to move it independently
- Wear it all the time. ~4 days to learn to eat with it. After a few days the jaw joint decompresses; when you take them out you can't get your back teeth together
- Phase 3: nighttime retention appliance keeping both upper and lower from relapsing
- Most cases: 18-24 months. Extreme cases: up to 5 years
- Moderate cases: $7,000-9,000. Severe cases: $15,000-20,000
