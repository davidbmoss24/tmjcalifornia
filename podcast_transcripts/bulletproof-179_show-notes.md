## Dr. Jennings' substantive teachings in this episode

### How he got into this work
Practiced general dentistry for 10 years. Studied European-type orthodontics (treating the jaw without braces). As his skills grew he took on more orofacial pain cases, and at the 10-year mark he sold the general practice and limited it to orofacial pain and dental orthopedics. At the time of recording (2014), he had been doing this work full-time for ~35 years.

### Scope of the problem in the general population
- The vast majority of modern humans are at least moderately compromised in their jaw alignment.
- **In excess of 75% of the population would benefit from treatment**, per Dr. Jennings' clinical estimate.
- Most patients arriving for treatment are off by about an eighth of an inch (~3mm) — not just a few thousandths.
- But once the bite is treated, **10,000ths of an inch is the working precision**. A cap re-glued by a non-specialist dentist can throw the whole system off and cause radiating pain from jaw to scalp to upper back within a day.

### The substance P mechanism (synthesized from this episode)
- The trigeminal sensory zone (ear to ear, crown to chin) has **100× more dense pain fibers than any other nerve in the body**.
- Dysfunction in that zone → elevated substance P.
- Substance P opens cell membranes (the "leaky cell" effect), making cells less efficient (impairs detox), hypersensitive (allergies/asthma/MCS), and prone to calcium influx (mitochondrial dysfunction — aerobic respiration shifts to anaerobic).
- It is a **major neurosecretory modulator** — throws off hormone levels and is a major modulator of movement (Torticollis, scoliosis named explicitly).
- Substance P controls **stem cell differentiation** — bone marrow (leukemia, lymphoma — some researchers think substance P is the sole cause of leukemia) and brain repair (you can't make new brain cells when it's elevated).

### The neural crest connection
Embryologically, beyond the standard endoderm/mesoderm/ectoderm, there's a fourth germ layer: **neural crest cells**. They form the most important parts of the nervous system, many cranial bones, and the **front four teeth**. Bite disturbances disproportionately impact neural-crest-derived structures.

Practical implication: when an adult's front four teeth take repeated impact from a misaligned bite, the nervous system gets hammered far more than expected.

### The Lovett Brothers / cervical-lumbar relationship
A chiropractic principle: if you twist the upper spine, there's a compensatory twist in the lower spine. The upper cervical typically becomes unstable as the jaw goes back; bringing the lower jaw forward stabilizes both upper and lower spinal regions. Low back pain, knee pain, even hip alignment can trace back to jaw misalignment.

### Sleep apnea (specific claims)
- 85% of sleep apnea is **obstructive** — the tongue falling into the airway at the back of the throat.
- When the airway is 50% reduced, **it takes ~15 years off your life** on average.
- Bringing the jaw forward brings the tongue with it and opens the airway.

### Treatment timeline & methodology
- Over 50% of pain patients out of pain within **48 hours**.
- Full treatment: typically 2 years for the majority. Severely shrunken/reduced jaws: 2–10 years (you have to grow the jaws bigger over time).
- Follow-up adjustments: every **4–6 weeks**.
- Materials: **polyvinyl siloxane** for bite checking — ~30× more accurate than carbon paper.
- Equipment (for dentists looking to do this work): **Myotronic** and **Bioresearch** make the neuromuscular dentistry instrumentation (jaw tracking with a magnet on the lower jaw, surface electromyography of temporalis and masseter, jaw-tracking noise analysis). Dr. Jennings has moved away from the equipment with experience but recommends it for newer practitioners.

### Why most dentists don't do this work
- 15 years before the recording, the ADA renamed orthodontists to "orthodontic and dento-facial orthopedic specialists" — **changed the title but not the educational requirements**. Most orthodontists drop the "dento-facial orthopedic" half from their actual titles.
- Most orthodontists still treat to the dental occlusal model — leaving an overbite in place — rather than to a functional model.
- You can't align the jaw with braces; if you strap all teeth together, the back teeth can't grow taller, which is the defect in almost every case.
- **Less than 5% of dentists** are trained in this approach. (Dr. Jennings says general dentists trying to do this work usually lack European-orthopedic-appliance training.)

### C fibers are polymodal (most medical schools miss this)
Standard teaching: C fibers carry pain and pressure signals. Dr. Jennings' point: C fibers are actually **polymodal** — they pick up radiation, electromagnetic fields, barometric pressure, **and body pH**. This is why EMF exposure can influence systemic substance P levels.

### Tinnitus
Hearing takes sensory input from all parts of the body, but the trigeminal is the predominant influence due to its density and proximity. Bite therapy is, in Dr. Jennings' view, **the most effective modality known for tinnitus** — higher than any other intervention.

### Substance P, knee/joint problems, sports performance
- **Medial knee pain** appears in roughly 30% of TMJ cases.
- Under Armour's research on bite-raising mouthguards showed: ~17% strength increase, improved endurance, reaction time, balance.
- An orthopedic surgeon who worked with the Padres and Chargers claimed **70% of sports injuries have a bite component**.
- Substance P controls the collagen matrix — so elevated substance P degrades ligament quality. People with Lyme or toxic mold (both substance-P-elevating) have ligament-tear problems.

### Lyme & viral susceptibility
- 95% of Lyme patients have TMJ problems — Dr. Jennings argues it's the TMJ causing the Lyme susceptibility, not the other way around. Substance P opens cell membranes wide enough to allow spirochete infiltration.
- A researcher at Children's Hospital Philadelphia has argued the same mechanism applies to HIV — closing those membranes (lowering substance P) should affect viral control.

### Brain blood flow and the prefrontal cortex
The trigeminal is a **major modulator of brain blood flow** — particularly to the prefrontal cortex. That's the "brain freeze" mechanism. Dr. Jennings ties this to autism, brain repair, glial cell function. Asprey notes that an Amen Clinic SPECT scan a dozen years earlier had shown him no metabolic activity in the prefrontal cortex — before his bite was realigned.

### Cognitive case stories shared in this episode

**Two 17-year-old boys with post-orthodontic cognitive collapse:**
- One: Two weeks after braces came off (after extractions had been done), he lost cognitive function. Couldn't do math, couldn't process. Got fuzzy, depressed, anxious. After two years of failed medical workups, Dr. Jennings repositioned his jaw forward. Four months without an episode (except when the mouthpiece came off).
- The other: Normal until age 12, fell off a cliff, became autistic — movement disorders, lost eye contact, severely anxious, placed in an impaired-autism school class. At age 17, exam revealed a 12-year molar had erupted in cross-bite. Put in a splint. Eye contact returned within a week. Vocabulary started returning within 3–4 weeks. More conversational, transferred care to an East Coast practitioner to continue.

**His own daughter:**
Severely retruded jaw at birth — major speech impediment, couldn't run. Started treatment at age 3. Brought her jaw forward, ended up slightly underbit, kept all her wisdom teeth (kept the smallest mouth he'd ever treated). Was eventually valedictorian. Dr. Jennings considers the bite work a contributing factor.

### Things that lower substance P
- Cayenne pepper (depletes it; people in hot climates use it medicinally) — caveats: 20% of people are nightshade-sensitive; commercial cayenne has aflatoxin/ochratoxin contamination issues; capsules + MCT-style oil increase bioavailability.
- All spices and antioxidants **mitigate** substance P (don't deplete it). Turmeric, ginger, vanilla. Polyphenols (coffee, chocolate).
- Vanilla is interesting — there are vanilloid receptors in the body; capsaicin is itself a vanilloid; most spices are vanilloid-derivative.
- 25% reduction in Parkinson's risk for people who eat peppers twice a week (University of Washington study).
