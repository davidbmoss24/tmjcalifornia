**On neuromuscular dentistry:**

Dr. Jennings: Dentistry historically has been based on occlusion, looking at it structurally — teeth supposed to fit together in a particular manner. Neuromuscular dentistry, invented in the late '60s in Seattle by Jankelson, adds another variable: muscles suspending the lower jaw. They look at where the muscles want the lower jaw to be, because the muscles have a very precise place in space where they're most happy. Muscles are strongest at their resting length, so they attach to your lower jaw to be strongest wherever they have to function most. For the vast majority of people, that's tip-to-tip — which conflicts with mainstream dental opinion.

All primitive humans bit end-on-end tip-to-tip. The vast majority of modern humans are over-bitten. Bites have been getting worse at a very fast rate for the last 200-300 years — soft diets, an industrialized diet. *Jaws: The Story of a Hidden Epidemic* (Sandra Kahn and Paul Ehrlich, ~2018) covers it.

**On substance P:**

Substance P is a neuropeptide, not a neurotransmitter — the difference being it has no reuptake mechanism. When secreted, it lingers and has endocrine-like effects. Discovered in 1931. ~25,000 articles on PubMed since 1980. Only one pharmaceutical, Emend (aprepitant), blocks substance P — approved only for treating nausea after chemotherapy. About $250 a pill. People are using it off-label for various conditions.

What it does: opens up cell membranes — creates "leaky gut" throughout the whole body. Lowers cell voltage potential, so cells fire too easily. As substance P goes up: more inflammatory conditions, neurological conditions, skin disorders. Smooth muscle effects: intermittent diarrhea, constipation.

Implicated as primary driver of:
- Most forms of cancer
- Mood disorders, depression, anger, violence, addiction, PTSD
- Fibromyalgia (3-5× normal in spinal fluid)
- Chronic fatigue (mitochondrial dysfunction)
- Allergies, asthma, autoimmune disorders

**On seizure disorders:**

Substance P is implicated as primary cause of most forms of seizures. Dr. Jennings has 80+ cases of grand mal seizures gone away with jaw orthopedic therapy.

**On neurodegenerative diseases:**

Substance P levels spike → brain cells fire too easily → cells burn out. Substance P also decreases nerve growth factor and controls stem cell differentiation, so brain repair is impaired. Long-term chronic bite dysfunction is likely a major factor in trauma incidences in pro sports and many neurodegenerative diseases.

**On psychiatrists / locus coeruleus:**

You'll find a few psychiatrists who've heard of substance P, but no functional psychiatrists actively applying this modality. None doing appropriate referrals and screening. They're treating it pharmaceutically or talking patients out of the problem.

Amen Clinics' SPECT scans look at regional cerebral blood flow — but the trigeminal nerve controls regional cerebral blood flow. The trigeminal vascular system is why a boxer gets hit in the jaw and passes out, why you get a brain freeze. Numerous cases of bite dysfunction leading to fainting episodes, cognitive dysfunction, fogginess, even TIA-like ischemic attacks and migrating paralysis. Dr. Jennings: "I've never had a neurologist in 40+ years consider trigeminal vascular as a mediator of TIA."

**On Covid and viral susceptibility:**

Substance P opens cell membranes and allows viral and spirochete infiltration. Dr. Wha (Children's Hospital Philadelphia) has published extensively claiming that if you could control substance P, you should be able to control HIV — viral entry depends on cell membrane porosity.

Long Covid: people had elevated substance P before they got Covid, still have it after the acute phase, leading to lingering symptoms.

Substance P also controls collagen matrix, so lung fibrosis, wound healing, all impaired.

**Closing point on screening:**

Stick your little finger in your ear and pull forward. You should NOT be able to feel your jaw joint opening and closing through your ear. A lot of people, when they bite down, their jaw joint goes way back into their ear space — associated with hearing loss, eustachian tube dysfunction. People have difficulty clearing with altitude, scuba diving, flying. Often a bite dysfunction issue.

Pigmentation disorders — substance P controls melanogenesis (stem cell differentiation). Case histories where pigmentation dysfunction normalized with bite therapy.
